Just in case, this is a long story. If you'd like to see a synthesized version, go to Linkedin

Due to my inclination toward the world of electronics and technology, I decided to avoid a traditional secondary education. I went to a technical school to grade with a specialization in electronics (or, as they say, an electronics technician). Thanks to the specific curricula, I got exposed to different programming languages from the beginning (even programming low-level PLCs).

After grading secondary school, I took a year off to develop a startup with two other friends from the technical school: STB Networks (or Santa Teresita Beach Networks, being Santa Teresita, the beach where we spent our summers as teenagers). We developed websites using Dreamweaver (including Macromedia Flash) and resold hosting plans. After two years, life took us on different paths. I got my first job as a Backend Software Developer, working with PHP and MySQL initially, and after including some Frontend coding some basic games done in Macromedia Flash with Actionscript. (the trendy "Full-stack" roles didn't exist back then üôÇ)

Recommendations from people I knew got me into a change where I forked my career into the SysAdmin world. I continued to develop some scripts here and there, but now I was administering Windows image deployment (RIS, anyone?), which then took me to complex architectures, mobile gateway deployments for MS Exchange, etc.

After a few years, I started looking for a change, and after being recommended by a colleague, I started my journey working in the "big leagues" with Avaya. I participated in the development from the ground up of a technical support team as a support engineer, troubleshooting complex customer environments composed of intertwined interactions between windows hosts, Linux hosts, and PBXs (including PostgreSQL databases and JTAPI applications). During this time, I experienced exponential growth in terms of technical knowledge. I also continued to do some shell scripting to automate routine tasks such as log collection and extraction from remote systems.

The team experience and dynamics got me to my first technical leadership role. I continued to perform technical support activities and trained newcomers to get them up to speed. Unfortunately, the company decided to close the office to move operations to another country.

I profited the time off to finish my Bachelor's of Science in Information Systems and then joined an airline company as an IT Operations Manager. From here onwards, my career took me into the Management world. Besides focusing on managerial tasks, I always found a spot to keep my "hands dirty." During this experience, I virtualized around 20 legacy servers running core services and many business applications with VMWare and built a parallel staging/prod cluster with XenServer for our Dev team.

In parallel, my wife received an offer to move to Switzerland, and we decided to go for it. I had to leave my job and get out to the Swiss market to find new challenges and opportunities to continue growing.
While searching, I worked for a few months with two new friends to help them out with their food delivery business. I profited and developed an online stock management solution with Google Apps Script (including recipe calculations, an auto PO generator based on the ingredients yield and email reporting, quality control for expiration dates, etc.). I then migrated the logic of the app into a fully fledged Backoffice website, using CakePHP/MySQL and bootstrap.

After a few months, I started working as a Customer Support & IT Ops manager on a swiss startup. I grew the Support Team globally, implementing new technologies and infrastructure to support business needs. Lately, I decided to go for a career change and take over a Senior manager role for different teams that operate cloud platforms.

Oh yes! and I forgot. I'm originally from Argentina and speak Spanish, English, French, and Italian.
